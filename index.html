MY STORY

Early Spark, Unconventional Path

My love for video games and anime wasn't just passive consumption; it ignited a creative fire at 11 years old. Online forums like Playstation Universe became my playground, not just for passionate console debates, but for crafting custom signatures, which became an artform.

That spark wasn't confined to pixels. College, while initially pursued, couldn't contain my hunger for growth. In 2016, I charted a new course, one paved with hackathon victories, immersive bootcamps, and freelance projects that transformed me into a seasoned web developer.

But my truest fulfillment found its roots in giving back. For four years, Detroit's youth became my students, entrusted with the keys to HTML, CSS, JavaScript, even Swift, AR, and Unreal Engine. Journi, the non-profit that embraced my passion, witnessed not only my students' growth, but the refinement of my own skills. It was an honor, yes, but more importantly, an act of love for my city, a love blossoming from those early pixels and signatures.
